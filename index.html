01 · Problem

A process everyone
hates but nobody fixed.

Getting a referral in India today means cold-messaging strangers on LinkedIn, getting ghosted, and repeating until exhausted. Employees who want to refer people are equally stuck.

For applicants
Search LinkedIn → cold DM employees → wait → get ignored → follow up awkwardly → no confirmation, no status, no closure. Repeat for every company.
For referrers
Post on LinkedIn → get 80 DMs in 48 hours → buried in feed → no way to shortlist, track, or follow up. Want to help but can't manage the chaos.
→

The gap: No neutral, structured platform exists where referrers post openings and applicants apply and track — outside of a company's internal HR tool. That's exactly what RefLink is.

02 · Solution

Structured referrals.
Both sides win.

RefLink replaces every awkward DM exchange with a clean, structured workflow. Referrers get a dashboard. Applicants get a tracking link. Nobody gets ghosted.

Post an opening
Employees post in minutes. Custom screening fields. Unique shareable link. Applications arrive in a dashboard — no DMs ever.
Apply with a form
Applicants apply without creating an account. Resume upload, cover note, custom questions. Confirmation email sent instantly.
Track to a clear outcome
5-state status tracker: Received → Under Review → Shortlisted → Referred / Not Moving Forward. No silence, ever.

5 insights for investors

01
Capturing existing behaviour, not creating new habit. 4M referral interactions happen every year in India tech with zero structure. Demand is proven. The tool just doesn't exist.
02
Tailwind, not headwind. India's tech job market grows 12–15% in 2026 with 125,000 new roles (NASSCOM). More openings = more referral posts = more demand for RefLink.
03
The monetisation bar is deliberately low. Base case — ₹10.8 Cr ARR — needs just 0.05% of India's 5.8M IT workforce as paying referrers. Conservative floor, not an optimistic ceiling.
04
Referrals are the highest-ROI hiring channel globally. 88% of employers rate referrals as best ROI. $7,500 saved per referral hire. Companies have strong incentive — RefLink rides that current.
05
Expansion is built in. This sizing is tech-only. Non-tech white-collar referrals (BFSI, SaaS, design, product) are the same problem at 3× scale. Year 3 expansion is organic.

Free

07 · Risks

What could go wrong.
What we're doing about it.

Every significant failure mode has a concrete mitigation built into the MVP — not a plan for later.

Risk
Fake posts — bad actors post non-existent jobs to harvest resumes.
Mitigation
LinkedIn OAuth verification at sign-up. Community flag button on every post. Manual review queue for flagged posts.
Risk
Referrer ghosting — posts open, applications received, then silence.
Mitigation
Auto email nudge after 5 days with no action. Posts auto-expire after 30 days of inactivity.
Risk
Spam applications — no-login forms are easy targets for bots and bulk appliers.
Mitigation
reCAPTCHA v2, honeypot field, and duplicate email+post check on every submission.
Risk
Cold start — platform launches empty, first visitors leave and don't return.
Mitigation
10–15 real seed posts from the founder's network go live before any public promotion. Non-negotiable.
Risk
LinkedIn builds this — they add a structured referral feature and out-distribute us overnight.
Mitigation
Move fast, build community trust and reputation scores before this is a threat. LinkedIn's incentive is feed engagement — not referral outcomes.
